Forces & Newton's Laws

Types of Forces
Every push or pull is a force. Forces have both magnitude and direction.
⬇️
Weight (W)
Gravitational pull on mass. W = mg. Always points downward.
⬆️
Normal (N)
Surface pushes back perpendicular to the contact surface.
↔️
Applied (Fapp)
External push or pull applied to an object.
⬅️
Friction (Ff)
Opposes relative motion. Acts parallel to surface.
🔗
Tension (T)
Force in ropes, strings, or cables pulling objects.
🌊
Drag (FD)
Air or fluid resistance opposing motion.
📐 Free-Body Diagram (FBD)

A FBD shows all forces acting ON a single object as arrows from a point or box. The length of each arrow represents magnitude.

↑ A box on a surface being pushed right with friction acting left.

⚖️ Balanced Forces

Net force = 0. Object stays still or moves at constant velocity. Equilibrium!

Fnet = 0 → a = 0

⚡ Unbalanced Forces

Net force ≠ 0. Object accelerates in direction of net force.

Fnet ≠ 0 → a = Fnet/m

Newton's Three Laws of Motion
Tap each example to reveal which law it demonstrates.
First Law
Law of Inertia
"An object at rest stays at rest, and an object in motion stays in motion at constant velocity, unless acted on by an unbalanced force."

Examples — click to confirm:

🚗 Car suddenly brakes — passengers lurch forward ⚽ Ball rolling on frictionless ice 📚 Book sitting still on a desk 🛸 Spacecraft coasting in space
Second Law
Fnet = ma
"The acceleration of an object is directly proportional to the net force and inversely proportional to its mass."

Examples — click to confirm:

🚀 Rocket burns fuel — accelerates faster as mass drops 🛒 Full shopping cart is harder to push 🏎️ F1 car — light body + huge engine = high acceleration
Third Law
Action & Reaction
"For every action there is an equal and opposite reaction force acting on a different object."

Examples — click to confirm:

🚀 Rocket exhaust pushes down → rocket goes up 🏊 Swimmer pushes wall → wall pushes swimmer 🦆 Bird wings push air down → air pushes bird up

Real-World Challenges
Apply Newton's Laws to solve these scenarios. Enter your answer and check!
⭐ Level 1
🛒 A shopping cart has a mass of 12 kg. A net force of 36 N acts on it.
What is the cart's acceleration?
m/s²
Use F = ma → a = F/m = 36/12
⭐ Level 1
🚗 A car of mass 1200 kg accelerates at 2.5 m/s².
What is the net force acting on it?
N
F = ma = 1200 × 2.5
⭐⭐ Level 2
📦 A box is pushed with 40 N to the right. Friction is 15 N to the left. The box has mass 5 kg.
What is the acceleration?
m/s²
Fnet = 40 − 15 = 25 N, then a = Fnet/m = 25/5
⭐⭐ Level 2
🏋️ A weightlifter holds a barbell of weight 600 N stationary above their head.
What is the force exerted by their hands on the barbell?
N
Object is stationary → balanced forces → Fhands = Weight = 600 N
⭐⭐⭐ Level 3
🚀 A rocket has a mass of 800 kg and its engines produce a thrust of 12000 N upward.
Given g = 10 m/s², what is the rocket's upward acceleration? (Hint: weight acts downward)
m/s²
W = 800×10 = 8000 N, Fnet = 12000 − 8000 = 4000 N, a = 4000/800 = 5 m/s²
